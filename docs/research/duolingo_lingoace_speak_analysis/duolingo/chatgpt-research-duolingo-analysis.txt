# Duolingo

**Feature Inventory:** Duolingo offers **100+ free courses in 40+ languages** (plus specialized courses like math and music)【25†L50-L57】.  Each language course is organized into a progression of **units and skills** covering practical communication goals【30†L108-L112】.  Lessons combine multiple exercise types: translation (often with images), multiple-choice, listening (audio), speaking (voice recognition), typing, and matching exercises【30†L108-L112】.  In large courses (e.g. Spanish, French) there are bonus activities like **Duolingo Stories** (short illustrated dialogues) and **DuoRadio podcast episodes** for extra listening practice【30†L139-L147】【27†L155-L160】.  For languages with non‑Latin scripts (Japanese, Korean, etc.), special “writing practice” lessons teach the characters【30†L162-L170】.  Duolingo includes built‑in **placement tests** so experienced users can skip ahead when they start【25†L79-L87】.  Other features include review drills in the Practice Hub, leaderboards by location, friend challenges, a social *Feed* with updates, and optional classroom tools (Duolingo for Schools offers teacher dashboards, group leaderboards, etc.).  The interface is richly gamified (see below).  

**User Journey:** New users first download the app or visit Duolingo’s site and **create an account**.  They pick a target language and answer onboarding questions (their goals, time commitment, and whether to enable reminders)【25†L75-L83】.  Duolingo then offers a choice: start from scratch or take a placement test【25†L79-L87】.  (If placed high, the user can still review skipped content but cannot reset to beginner mode.)  Once set up, the learner sees a **course tree** of units.  The first lesson is usually very basic (e.g. teaching a few key words or phrases with images and audio).  As the learner completes each small lesson, the app awards XP points and unlocks the next node.  A profile page shows streak count (consecutive days) and Duolingo Score.  From day one, Gamification elements appear: a daily goal reminder, heart (life) count, and the option to compete on weekly XP leaderboards【27†L185-L193】【30†L194-L202】.  (If the user misses a day, they may lose their streak unless they use a “Streak Freeze” from the shop.)  Over time the learner is prompted to engage with practice activities (to strengthen weak skills) and bonus content (Stories, podcasts) as it becomes available.  The user can add friends and see a feed of their progress, or join leagues and friendly challenges.  Throughout this journey, Duolingo periodically shows encouraging messages or streak reminders (e.g. “You’re on fire!”)【72†L156-L164】 to motivate continued use.

**Lesson/Curriculum Structure:** Each course is structured as a **tree of thematic units** – for example “Basics,” “Greetings,” “Food,” etc. – and learners unlock them sequentially【30†L108-L112】.  Within each unit, lessons are very short (“bite-sized”), focusing on just a few vocabulary or grammar points per lesson【30†L114-L122】.  Exercises in early lessons start very simple (often matching words to pictures) and become progressively harder (e.g. eventually requiring the user to *type out* a full sentence)【30†L117-L123】.  The system uses spaced repetition: completed lessons reappear as practice lessons later, targeting mistakes or “stale” items【30†L119-L123】.  Thus Duolingo **adapts to the learner** by mixing review questions on words/concepts that need reinforcement with new material.  For most major languages, curriculum follows common standards (e.g. it covers roughly A1–B2 CEFR levels) – the company claims that 5 completed sections on Duolingo is roughly equivalent to 5 college semesters【30†L100-L108】.  There is no live instructor: the app is entirely **self‑paced and automated**.  (However, Duolingo offers a **virtual teacher** in its new Max subscription – an AI avatar named Lily – to role-play conversations【32†L234-L240】【70†L82-L90】.)  Each lesson typically takes just a few minutes.  After each lesson the user returns to the tree view and taps the next available lesson.

**Gamification & Retention:** Gamification is core to Duolingo’s design【30†L185-L194】.  Key mechanics include:
- **XP & Leaderboards:** Completing any activity (lesson, story, challenge) earns XP.  Users join a weekly *League* leaderboard against other learners worldwide or among friends【30†L185-L193】.  Winning a league promotes you to a higher tier; you can opt out if you prefer.  
- **Daily Streak:** A flaming streak counter increases by one for each day the user completes at least one lesson【30†L194-L199】. Streaks motivate return visits.  The app sells **Streak Freezes** (using gems) to preserve streak if you miss a day【30†L194-L199】.  
- **Duolingo Score:** A course progress score (visible after each unit) lets users compare progress across languages or with friends【30†L201-L204】.  
- **Hearts (Lives):** By default learners have a limited number of hearts (lives) representing allowed mistakes.  Each wrong answer costs a heart, and running out forces a review session【30†L221-L226】.  (Subscribers get unlimited hearts.)  Hearts can be refilled with earned gems or by practicing old lessons.  
- **Gems/Currency:** Correct answers and milestones reward virtual currency (“gems”)【30†L206-L209】.  Gems can be spent in the in-app Shop on power-ups (e.g. double XP timers, bonus skills, extra streak freezes).  
- **Achievements & Avatar:** There is an achievement (badge) system for hitting milestones (longest streak, total XP, etc.).  Duolingo’s mascot **Duo the Owl** and other characters give encouragement, making it feel game‑like.  
- **Social Features:** Users can add **Friends**, start Friend streaks, or send encouraging notes【30†L211-L214】. A *Feed* tab shows friend activity and Duolingo announcements【30†L216-L219】.  
These features create a compelling loop: earning XP and rewards and seeing progress (and social rank) gives users short-term goals to keep practicing daily【27†L185-L194】【72†L156-L164】.

**Monetization & Pricing:** Duolingo is fundamentally **freemium**【11†L334-L338】.  All core lessons and features can be used for free (with ads), but certain conveniences require payment.  Two paid tiers exist【32†L234-L240】【32†L242-L246】: 
- **Super Duolingo (formerly Plus):** ~\$14.99/month (with family plans up to 6 users).  Removes all ads, grants unlimited hearts (no lesson interruption), extra streak freezes, and an enhanced Practice Hub for focused review【32†L234-L240】. 
- **Duolingo Max:** ~\$19.99/month (or higher in some markets).  Includes all Super benefits plus new AI features powered by GPT-4: *Lily Video Call* (conversational practice with an AI tutor) and *Roleplay* chat scenarios with on-screen characters【32†L242-L246】【70†L82-L90】.  (“Explain My Answer” detailed feedback is also advertised in Max.) 
Free users can use Duolingo indefinitely but see occasional ads (between lessons) and have the aforementioned heart limits.  Duolingo emphasizes that paid subscriptions help fund the free mission【11†L334-L338】.

**Target Audience:** Duolingo is aimed at **casual language learners**, not intensive academicians【72†L156-L164】.  Its design explicitly targets **beginners and hobbyists** – e.g. travelers, school students, or “people with five spare minutes”【72†L156-L164】.  The youthful, game-like UX (cartoon mascot, colorful interface) appeals especially to teens and young adults, though many adults use it too.  Its marketing slogan is “free, fun, and effective” learning【25†L50-L57】.  Duolingo is used by students of all ages; the company also offers *Duolingo for Schools* so teachers can assign and monitor progress in class.  In sum, Duolingo’s audience is broad, but its products are optimized for anyone looking for a self‑paced, daily-learning app rather than a formal classroom course【72†L156-L164】【25†L50-L57】.
