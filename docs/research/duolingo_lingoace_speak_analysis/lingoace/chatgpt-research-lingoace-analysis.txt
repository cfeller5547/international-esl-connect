# LingoAce

**Feature Inventory:** LingoAce is a live‑class tutoring platform (web and app) primarily for **children (ages ~3–15)** learning Chinese, English, Math, and even Music【23†L39-L41】【52†L63-L71】.  Its core offerings are scheduled online classes (typically 25 or 55 minutes long) taught **live by certified bilingual teachers** (college‑educated, fluent in Chinese and English for Chinese courses)【13†L65-L72】【52†L63-L71】.  Users can take **1-on-1 sessions or small group classes** (class size often capped around 4–6) depending on the subject. LingoAce provides a **structured curriculum** tailored to each age/level: e.g. for Chinese it has four tiers (Preschool for ages 3–5, Core for ages 5–15 beginners, Discovery for intermediate, and Advanced)【13†L79-L87】.  The curriculum aligns with formal standards: Chinese courses follow HSK and AP Chinese guidelines, English with CEFR standards, and Math with U.S. Common Core (Singapore Math method)【52†L71-L75】【52†L81-L87】.  Lesson content is highly interactive – using digital courseware with games, songs, stories, and picture books【52†L71-L75】【13†L89-L92】.  For example, LingoAce highlights “interactive courseware with games” and *storytelling, gamified practice, and closed-loop review* for English classes【52†L71-L75】【52†L75-L82】.  Homework is an integral feature: each class often ends with assigned practice (online exercises or printable worksheets) that students upload for teacher review【13†L89-L94】.  After every session, teachers provide personalized feedback and progress reports.  The platform also offers class scheduling tools (parents can easily book or cancel classes online) and the ability to **record classes** for review【13†L89-L94】【13†L141-L150】.  Some schools and parents use LingoAce’s reporting and certificate system to track student achievements (informal completion certificates may be issued after each level).  

**User Journey:** Prospective users visit LingoAce’s site or app and typically **book a free trial class**.  (The website prominently offers a “free VIP trial class” sign-up form.) During the trial, the student meets a teacher; afterwards a curriculum advisor or director will discuss the child’s level and set an appropriate class plan【53†L7-L10】.  In one parent review, after the trial they consulted with the curriculum director and decided on a twice-weekly intermediate course for the student【53†L7-L10】.  The user then purchases a package of classes.  Parents and students log into the platform to **schedule their live classes** at convenient times (the interface allows easy rescheduling or cancellation, which many users praise【13†L139-L147】). When class time arrives, the student joins a video classroom through the app or browser: a typical lesson has the teacher sharing an interactive whiteboard/slide deck with the student, playing songs, showing games, or asking the student to respond.  During class, the teacher awards stars or thumbs-up for correct answers to gamify the session【13†L89-L92】【13†L141-L150】.  After class, any assigned homework is reviewed online.  Over time, parents and students can see completed lesson counts, upcoming schedule, and teacher comments in their dashboard.  Ongoing usage means logging in for each scheduled session; there are no daily requirements, but many students follow 1–3 classes per week depending on their plan.  

**Lesson/Curriculum Structure:** LingoAce’s content is **level-based and synchronous** (taught live rather than self-paced modules).  For Chinese, the four-tier structure (Preschool, Core, Discovery, Advanced) progresses from alphabet/foundations to high-level proficiency【13†L79-L87】.  English courses are similarly graded (often by age groups or CEFR levels); descriptions mention using chants, props and games for young learners to build fluency.  Math courses follow a structured sequence (building arithmetic to advanced problem-solving) based on Singapore Math.  Each lesson slot is either 25 or 55 minutes (parents choose when booking), and each covers a few key concepts with a mix of activities (e.g. a new dialog with vocabulary, then related games).  The learning is **teacher-led**, so difficulty is adjusted on the fly: if a student struggles, the teacher can simplify, and can advance faster if the student excels.  The curriculum often uses real-world contexts – for example, Chinese lessons use picture books and dialogues relevant to daily life.  After several lessons (usually 10–20), a student is evaluated and can move to the next level.  Certification of completion is informal, but students often earn a certificate when they finish a level.  

**Gamification & Retention:** Compared to Duolingo, LingoAce’s gamification is modest and built into the live class experience.  Teachers frequently use **virtual stars and thumbs-up** to praise correct answers and encourage students【13†L89-L92】【13†L141-L150】.  Parents report that even simple rewards like stickers or star icons from the teacher greatly motivate young children【13†L141-L150】.  The courses themselves include playful elements (songs, chants, stories, and in-class games), and lessons end with fun exercises to keep kids engaged.  LingoAce also sends reminders and progress reports to parents (not just to students) so families stay on track.  There are no formal point systems, streak counters, or public leaderboards – the retention strategy relies on regular scheduling and the personal bond with the teacher.  Because each class is “live,” students tend to stick to their chosen schedule to avoid losing prepaid class credits.  

**Monetization & Pricing:** LingoAce is entirely **paid-tuition**.  There is no ad-supported free content – users pay for each class.  Pricing is transparent by subject and level (the site lists exact per-lesson rates).  According to user reports, a 25-minute class costs on the order of **\$15** and a 55-minute class about **\$30**【13†L121-L129】 (rates may vary by location and level).  Bulk purchases (monthly, semi-annual, annual plans) come with discounts, and occasional promotions are offered【13†L121-L129】.  Typically, payment is per class or as a block of lessons.  There is no subscription model in the traditional sense – instead, families maintain an account credit of class tokens.  LingoAce heavily promotes its **free trial lesson** as a way to convert users; after the trial, the platform encourages parents to buy packages of classes.  There is also a “family plan” concept, but it mainly refers to scheduling multiple children’s classes rather than a price reduction.  

**Target Audience:** LingoAce is explicitly designed for **children and young teens** and their parents【52†L63-L71】【13†L79-L87】.  Its marketing and UX reflect an educational, parent‑friendly tone (calm colors, cartoon mascots) with interactive child‑oriented content.  The main demographic is **school-age kids (roughly ages 3–15)** who need supplemental academic or conversational language instruction【52†L63-L71】.  For example, the Chinese program is aimed at kids preparing for exams or bilingual proficiency (aligned with HSK/AP)【13†L79-L87】【52†L71-L75】, and the English program targets primary-level learners using songs and Total Physical Response methods【52†L75-L82】.  Marketing emphasizes certified teachers and curriculum rigor, which appeals to parents seeking real improvement rather than casual learners.  In summary, LingoAce’s users are typically children in elementary or middle school, or teens, learning Chinese (most commonly) or English/Math as part of their academic studies. Its style is educational (not game-like), and its retention comes from classroom-style structure rather than game rewards.
